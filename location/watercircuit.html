Magic Water Circuit

The Magic Water Circuit (Circuito Mágico del Agua) is one of Lima's most popular attractions and a must-see for visitors to Peru's capital city. Located in the Parque de la Reserva, this impressive park is home to the world's largest fountain complex, a title it holds in the Guinness World Records.

Opened in 2007, the park features a series of beautiful fountains, each with its own unique design and lighting effects. At night, the fountains come alive with colorful lights, music, and choreographed water shows that attract both locals and tourists alike.

The park features over a dozen distinct fountains, including the famous Fantasy Fountain, where water dances in time with music and lights, creating a spectacular show. Visitors can also experience interactive fountains, such as the Labyrinth of Dreams, where guests attempt to navigate through a maze of water jets without getting wet.

Highlights of the Magic Water Circuit include:

1. The Fantasy Fountain: The centerpiece of the park, featuring nightly multimedia shows with music, lasers, and images projected onto the water.

2. The Tunnel of Surprises: A 35-meter-long water tunnel that visitors can walk through while being surrounded by arches of water.

3. The Labyrinth of Dreams: A fun and interactive fountain maze that challenges visitors to dodge streams of water.

The Magic Water Circuit is an ideal destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike. The combination of music, technology, and traditional Peruvian motifs in the water shows makes it a unique cultural experience.

Located just minutes from Lima's historic center, the park is easily accessible by taxi or public transport. It's open in the afternoons and evenings, with the best time to visit being after sunset when the light and water displays are most vibrant.
