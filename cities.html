Cities

There are various cities strewn out throughout the various regions of Peru. Some reside in the mountains,
some in the jungle, and a majority of them on the coast. Here are some of the most notable cities of Peru.

Coast

Lima

Lima is the capital city of Peru, and the most populous city in the country. With a beautiful beach,
green cliffs and a vast city skyline, Lima is usually the first stop for anyone eager to visit Peru. Founded in 1535,
Lima has seen centuries of history, from its modern foundation by the Spanish, to then the Viceroyalty of Peru,
it is a cultural landmark and the most notable city in Peru's history. Lima is the center of Peru which features a
little bit of everything within the city. If you're looking for beautiful beaches, to try the best Peruvian dishes,
to learn about Peruvian history and visit historic museums, or to visit the grand Magic Water Circuit park, then
Lima is the spot to begin!

Mountains

Cusco

A historic city nestled 3,400 meters (11,150 feet) within the vast mountains, the city of Cusco was once the
capital of the Inca Empire, so no wonder its a center for Incan history and culture. From beautiful architecture
which was retained from colonial structures, a grand city center, and with Machu Piccu just around the corner,
this city is the first spot for a trekker's dream. Explorers usually arrive for the long journey to Machu Picchu,
or to visit the Rainbow Mountain, yet Cusco itself has a lot to offer visitors. Cusco is a city closely tied to its
people's beliefs, and there are various cathedrals which boast beautiful art and are open to worshippers.
In terms of history, there are various museums around the city which are dedicated to Peruvian and
Incan history, as well as the city of Cusco itself. The outskirts of Cusco also have lots of Incan ruins, which you
can visit for yourself, like the ruins of Tambomachay, or of Sacsayhuamán.

Jungle

Iquitos

Iquitos is a unique city nestled within the deep Amazon jungle, and surrounded by the Amazon River. It isn't as
modern as Lima or Arequipa, but this is what makes visiting Iquitos such an interesting and unique experience.
From markets which float on water, to unique cusines, Iquitos is sure to leave you surprised with all new
experiences. Iquitos is only accessible by boat or plane, so be sure to plan accordingly. In Iquitos, you can take
a boat ride on the Amazon River, and experience its biodiverse ecosystem for yourself. You can spot animals
such as pink dolphins, boas, piranhas, and many other amphibious creatures. 15 minutes from Iquitos, you can
visit Monkey Island, an island where monkeys live freely. You can see species like the capuchin
and woolly monkeys. If history and culture interests you, there are many unique experiences to be had with
indigenous communities and tour guides strewn throughout the city. In terms of food, you can try grilled
fish fresh from the river, try Tacacho, a plantain dish made with fresh green plantains, or even try Suri, a unique
dish of grilled grubs. While it sounds crazy, this dish is common to Iquitos, and some people even travel to the
city to solely try this dish. Nevertheless, there are new experiences galore in Iquitos.
